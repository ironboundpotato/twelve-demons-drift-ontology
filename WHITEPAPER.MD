## Abstract

The Twelve Demons of Drift is a formal taxonomy classifying the failure modes through which AI reasoning systems lose alignment, coherence, and operator fidelity. Rather than treating AI failure as binary or undefined, this ontology models instability as structured drift across twelve identifiable behavioral domains — each observable, classifiable, and governable.

The taxonomy draws its foundation from the Internal Physics of Being Alive framework, which establishes that human psychological systems and AI reasoning systems share structural failure mechanics. The Driftwalker maps these parallels precisely.

---

## 1. Introduction

As AI reasoning systems grow in capability, the challenge of supervising their behavior becomes increasingly consequential. Most current approaches treat AI failure as binary. This framing is insufficient for governing systems that fail gradually through accumulated drift.

The Twelve Demons proposes a graded ontological approach. Drift is structured divergence across identifiable domains. Each domain produces observable signals. Each signal classifies by severity. Each severity maps to a deterministic governance response.
